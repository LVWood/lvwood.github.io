Website Redesign

J&J Lubrano Music Antiquarians


THE BRIEF: redesign a pre-existing commercial website in order to modernize the firm's branding and online presence. A well-respected company with a loyal but aging clientele, they needed an updated online presence in order to satisfy a new generation of clients.


REWARDS: for a venerable antiquarian firm with over 40 years in the business, it was a rewarding challenge to freshen and simplify the site while maintaining familiar brand identity.


CHALLENGES: the site's pre-existing branding (logo, colors) were used haphazardly and without a formal style-guide. It relied on low-quality, non-scalable images and had not been updated in some time. Additionally, the site was hosted and managed by a 3rd party company and any design updates would need to adhere to the template and data structure already in use.

Branding & Site Design

Ignea Strata


THE BRIEF: design and build a fully responsive website for a new, independent music ensemble. The site needs to establish the group's personality and aesthetic and provide a space for marketing and promotional materials.


REWARDS: with beautiful photo assets and a sophisticated aesthetic, the site lent itself to a simple, clean design with plenty of room to develop over time.


CHALLENGES: wide-angle photos with the subjects spaced far apart was lovely for landscape designs, but required some creative solutions to present the images effectively for mobile.

The mobile version stacks the animated banner text.

The desktop about page features a wide-angle shot of the ensemble with parallax scrolling.

In the mobile version, parallax is disabled and the wide-angle image is cropped into two sections featuring closeups of the singers.
