Design, Layout & Photography

J & J Lubrano Sales Catalogues

THE BRIEF: Update the presentation of sales catalogues, curated collections of carefully researched and assessed antiquarian musical items.


CHALLENGES: While the firm's previous publications were full of rigorous research and contained highly valuable materials, relatively little effort or time was invested toward presenting the material in a visually stimulating way or toward curating the material beyond simple alphabetical lists.
In my designs, careful efforts were made to create clear typographical hierarchy for easy, at-a-glance comprehension of what could be extremely dense, wordy descriptions. Whitespace, typographical consistency, and carefully planned placement of images and text were priorities in my designs.


REWARDS: Presenting material of great artistic, historic, and cultural value was a pleasure. The autograph manuscripts, first editions, letters, sketches, and rare musical works that I worked with deserved visual presentation as beautiful as the works themselves and it was gratifying to design catalogues that were as thoughtfully composed as their contents deserved.

The new catalogues maintained certain brand identity markers through color and typography, but were largely designed to showcase and complement the particular styles of featured inventory. The primary goal was to design catalogues stylistically curated to match the contents.


For comparison, see some examples of the firm's previous catalogues here and here.


In addition to better curating the material, the redesigned catalogues were more interactive, with an internally hyperlinked table of contents and active links between the catalogue inventory numbers and the sales website.
